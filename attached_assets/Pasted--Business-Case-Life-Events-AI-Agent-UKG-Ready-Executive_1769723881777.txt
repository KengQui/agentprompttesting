## Business Case: Life Events AI Agent (UKG Ready)

### Executive summary

Employees frequently need help with **Qualifying Life Events (QLEs)**—marriage, birth/adoption, loss of coverage, moves, divorce—yet the process is time-sensitive and policy-driven. A **Life Events AI Agent** for UKG Ready will provide 24/7, consistent guidance, reduce HR/Benefits ticket volume, improve submission completeness (correct event type/date/docs), and reduce missed deadlines and coverage gaps. The agent uses a **customer-specific policy table** (uploaded/mocked for prototype; derived from configuration in production) to avoid inaccurate guidance.

---

### Problem / opportunity

**Common pain points**

* Employees don’t know if they qualify, what they can change, what documents are required, or how effective dates work.
* HR/Benefits gets repetitive, high-volume questions and “status check” follow-ups.
* Incomplete/incorrect submissions (wrong event type, missing dates/docs) cause back-and-forth, delays, and denials.
* Missed submission windows lead to exceptions and unhappy employees—sometimes resulting in coverage gaps.

**Why it matters**

* QLEs are high-impact moments; delays and confusion create strong negative employee experience.
* HR/Benefits time is expensive; QLE inquiries are predictable and largely standardized.

---

### Proposed solution

A **Life Events AI Agent** embedded in the employee support experience (or prototype app for usability tests) that:

1. **Understands the employee’s situation** and maps it to a QLE category
2. Uses a **customer-specific policy table** (or handbook excerpts) as source of truth for:

   * submission window
   * allowed changes
   * documentation requirements
   * effective date rules and approval steps
3. **Asks minimal intake questions** (event date, who is affected, desired changes, benefits impacted, docs available)
4. Provides **step-by-step guidance** to complete the process (system-agnostic in prototype; ESS steps in production)
5. **Escalates** when policy is unclear or exceptions are required, with a structured handoff summary

---

### Target users

* **Employees (ESS)** needing QLE help
* **HR/Benefits** teams handling escalations and exceptions
* **Payroll/Benefits admins** (indirectly) through reduced rework and cleaner submissions

---

### Scope (MVP)

**In scope**

* Common life events: marriage, divorce, birth, adoption/placement, loss/gain of other coverage, move affecting plan availability, death of dependent/spouse, dependent aging out
* Document guidance (what is typically needed + customer-specific rules if available)
* Deadline/window guidance and “outside window” escalation handling
* Effective date explanations (policy-driven; no promises if unknown)
* Handoff summary generation

**Out of scope (guardrails)**

* Legal advice (divorce/custody)
* Tax advice (what to claim)
* Handling coverage disputes beyond explanation (route to HR/Benefits)

---

### Benefits and value

**Employee experience**

* Faster answers, fewer missed windows, clearer next steps
* Lower anxiety during major life changes

**Operational efficiency**

* Ticket deflection for repetitive “how do I / do I qualify / what docs / when effective” inquiries
* Reduced HR follow-ups due to improved intake completeness and fewer incorrect submissions

**Risk reduction**

* Consistent, policy-aware guidance decreases incorrect recommendations
* Structured escalation reduces sensitive-data handling and improves compliance

---

### Metrics / KPIs

* **Ticket deflection rate** for QLE-related inquiries
* **First-contact resolution rate** (issues resolved without HR follow-up)
* **Submission completeness rate** (event date present, correct event type, docs attached when required)
* **Time-to-submit** (from question to successful submission)
* **Reduction in “outside window” exception requests**
* Employee **CSAT** for benefits support

---

### ROI model (how to justify)

You can estimate ROI with a simple formula:

**Annual savings** ≈
`(QLE inquiry volume per year) × (deflection %) × (avg minutes per ticket) × (HR hourly cost / 60)`

Example inputs to plug in (use your own):

* QLE inquiries/year: 5,000
* Deflection: 30–50%
* Avg handling time: 8–12 minutes
* HR cost: $40–$60/hr

Also consider soft savings:

* fewer retroactive corrections
* fewer employee escalations
* reduced carrier/broker back-and-forth due to missing docs

---

### Risks and mitigations

**Risk:** Customer policies vary; incorrect guidance harms trust

* **Mitigation:** Policy table as source of truth; “don’t guess” rule; escalate when missing

**Risk:** Users ask for legal/tax advice

* **Mitigation:** hard guardrails + redirect to HR/professionals

**Risk:** Document handling / sensitive data

* **Mitigation:** do not accept sensitive docs in chat; use secure upload channels

---

### Implementation approach

**Prototype**

* Use mock customer policy tables (Strict/Moderate/Lenient) + sample employee dataset
* Evaluate usability: clarity, trust, completion success, escalation flow

**Production (future)**

* Replace mocked policy with:

  * configuration-derived policy table (where available), and/or
  * customer handbook/SPD ingestion + validation
* Add telemetry: what users ask, where they drop off, escalation reasons
